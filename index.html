About

Video Ports

Description

In computer hardware, a port serves as an interface between the computer and other computers or peripheral devices. In computer terms, a port generally refers to the part of a computing device available for connection to peripherals such as input and output devices.

Functions

These ports are typically found in computers, monitors, TVS and various video equipments. The most common functions are to send analog video signals or send and receive digital signals between the computer and the deployment devices, with the aim of allowing the user to view the content.

Curiosity

VGA was initially created by IBM for their x86 machines. It worked so well that VGA went on to become an industry standard on computers, televisions, projectors, and other electronics. It was created in 1987 and today it is used

Types

Some Video Ports

HDMI

High Definition Multimedia Interface. It is one of the newest and common digital connectors today, HDMI can be found not only in computers and monitors, but notebooks, televisions, and home theater equipment as well. Reasons for its popularity is its ability to carry both uncompressed digital video and audio signals

VGA

Video Graphics Array. It is the oldest video connector still in use today, this connector was the standard for connecting monitors to computers. Unlike modern connectors such as HDMI and DisplayPort that carry digital signals, VGA carries analog data only. While VGA can still be found on some new computers, it is slowly being phased out for newer digital connectors.

DVI

Digital Visual Interface. There are three variants:
DVI-A - Supports Analog Signal Only
DVI-D - Supports Digital Signal Only
DVI-I - Supports Analog and Digital Signals. The DVI standard provides the option of dual link, this connectors contain an additional six pins that can support to a maximum resolution of 2560 x 1600 at 60 Hertz.

DisplayPort

Is commonly used to handle digital video signals, it can also carry digital audio and USB data as well. One of the main advantages of DisplayPort is its support for multi-monitors. One DisplayPort output can drive up to three monitors, depending on the video resolution, through a daisy-chain connection. Additionally, DisplayPort connectors have a locking mechanism to prevent the cable from disconnecting accidentally.

USB-C

USB-C (properly known as USB Type-C; commonly known as just Type-C) is a 24-pin USB connector system with a rotationally symmetrical connector. The designation C refers only to the connector's physical configuration or form factor. It can transmit data, video, audio and power (between 60W and 100W), so it is considered a multi-purpose port.

Components Video

A component video connection transmits a high-quality analog video signal between devices. The component video ports are the red, blue, and green AV ports on your TV, and before HDMI became popular, they offered the highest video quality.
